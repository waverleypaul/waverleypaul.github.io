About

I am an independent economic researcher and consultant specialising in the use of data analytics and economic modelling to generate insights that support informed decision-making. I use R programming to transform datasets into clear, visual narratives; uncovering the underlying stories that make data actionable.

Drawing on an 18-year career at the Eastern Caribbean Central Bank, I bring extensive expertise in developing evidence-based policy recommendations, preparing technical reports, and compiling and analysing monetary and financial statistics to support strategic decision-making.

As an independent researcher, I combine deep institutional experience with modern analytical tools to transform data into actionable insights designed to strengthen financial systems, support sustainable economic development and guide policymakers and organisations.

I am available for remote consulting engagements, freelance research contracts, and collaborative projects in financial sector analysis, macroprudential policy research, and data visualisation.

Research Areas

Macroprudential Policy and Financial Development

Evaluating financial systems and structural economic trends.

My work focuses on analysing financial sectors and macroeconomic data to evaluate performance, assess systemic risks, and design forward-looking frameworks. I translate empirical findings into actionable policy insights that mitigate systemic vulnerabilities, safeguard financial stability, and support sustainable economic growth.

Panel Data Analysis

Investigating economic drivers across multi-dimensional datasets.

I apply econometric methodologies, with a particular focus on panel data models, to identify key economic relationships over time and across entities. I emphasise robust, reproducible coding practices to ensure that statistical outputs are reliable, transparent, and suitable for policy design.

Data Visualisation Using R

Exploring complex datasets to uncover actionable narratives.

I leverage the R ecosystem to explore data dynamically and transform statistical outputs into intuitive visual narratives that make complex insights accessible to policymakers and stakeholders.

Due to the confidential and proprietary nature of central banking policy work, the technical reports, policy briefings, and analytical frameworks I developed during my 18+ years at the Eastern Caribbean Central Bank are not publicly available.

My current independent research draws on open-source macroeconomic and financial data to explore financial sector resilience, systemic risks, and generate policy-relevant insights.
